# Medically Unlikely Edits (MUE)

## What an MUE is

A Medically Unlikely Edit is the maximum number of units of service for a single
HCPCS or CPT code that a provider would report under most circumstances for one
patient on one date of service. MUEs exist to reduce errors from typographical
mistakes, unit-versus-time confusion, and anatomic impossibilities. Not every code
has an MUE, and the absence of an MUE does not mean any number of units is payable.

## MUE Adjudication Indicator (MAI)

The MAI describes how the cap is enforced and whether it can be challenged:

- MAI 1 is a claim line edit. The cap applies to a single claim line. Units above
  the cap on one line are denied, but clinically appropriate distinct services may
  sometimes be reported across multiple lines with suitable modifiers.
- MAI 2 is a date-of-service edit based on policy. It is an absolute cap grounded
  in anatomy or coding policy and cannot be bypassed. Units above the cap are
  denied and the denial is not subject to appeal for that reason.
- MAI 3 is a date-of-service edit based on clinical judgment. Units above the cap
  are denied on the initial claim, but payment may be possible on appeal when the
  documentation establishes medical necessity for the higher number of units.

## Reporting above an MUE

When the units actually furnished legitimately exceed an MAI 1 or MAI 3 cap,
report the service in a way that reflects the distinct units and retain
documentation. For an MAI 2 cap, exceeding the value is treated as incorrect
coding and will be denied regardless of documentation.
