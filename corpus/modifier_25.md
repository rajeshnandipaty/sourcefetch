# Modifier 25 — Significant, Separately Identifiable E/M Service

> Illustrative reference text for demonstration. Not authoritative or current.

## Definition

Modifier 25 identifies a significant, separately identifiable evaluation and
management (E/M) service performed by the same provider on the same day as a
procedure or other service. It is appended to the E/M code, not to the procedure.

## When it applies

A minor procedure includes the inherent evaluation and management work needed to
perform it. Reporting an E/M service in addition to the procedure is appropriate
only when the E/M work goes above and beyond the usual pre- and post-procedure
care. The patient's condition must require a separately identifiable E/M service
beyond the decision to perform the procedure.

## Documentation

The record should clearly show two distinct services: the procedure note and a
separately documented history, examination, and medical decision making that
stands on its own. A separate diagnosis is not required to use modifier 25, but
the documentation must support that the E/M service was significant and separate.

## Common errors

Appending modifier 25 to every visit that includes a minor procedure is a frequent
source of denials and audit findings. The modifier should not be used when the only
service was the procedure itself, or when the E/M work was limited to the routine
assessment associated with performing that procedure.
